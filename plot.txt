Andrzej Sapkowski fights with CDProject over the legendary “Money from the Mountain,” a fortune earned from stories about witchers and monsters.
The Guild of Red Projects claims they transformed forgotten tales into glory across the Continent.
Sapkowski insists the mountain would never exist without the stories he created in the first place.
Their dispute divides kingdoms, merchants, bards, and even witchers themselves.
Rumors spread that deep beneath the mountain sleeps an ancient spirit feeding on greed and ambition.
When accountants and negotiators begin disappearing, both sides secretly hire a witcher to investigate.
The witcher discovers the conflict is awakening a cursed force born from broken contracts and stolen legacy.
As tensions rise, the Guild wants profit, Sapkowski wants recognition, and the kings want control of the mountain’s gold.
The witcher must decide whether art belongs to its creator or to those who made it famous.
In the end, both sides reach an uneasy agreement, but the mountain never stops whispering for more gold.
